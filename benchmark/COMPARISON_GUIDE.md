# Visual Comparison Guide

Use this guide when comparing an original reel with a generated Veo result.

## Creator Vibe

- Does the generated subject feel like the same type of creator?
- Is the confidence, directness, or softness of the performance preserved?
- Does the result feel social-native rather than generic stock footage?

## Motion Cadence

- Does the generated motion match the original energy level?
- Does movement feel continuous, rhythmic, static, energetic, or restrained in the right way?
- Are gestures, posing, preparation, or athletic movements represented at the correct intensity?

## Social-Media Realism

- Does the video feel like a reel rather than a cinematic ad unless the source was ad-like?
- Is the framing plausible for creator content?
- Does it preserve influencer, tutorial, product, food, travel, or sports conventions?

## Camera Feel

- Does the generated camera match the original camera energy?
- Is it static, handheld, selfie-like, tracking, close-up, or product-focused in a plausible way?
- Is there excessive cinematic movement not supported by the source?

## Temporal Pacing

- Does the generated moment unfold at the right speed?
- Does it preserve the reel's sense of progression?
- Does it avoid feeling frozen when the source is energetic?

## Expression Energy

- Does facial expression, posture, and body language match the source mood?
- Does the creator feel camera-aware when the source does?

## Aesthetic Similarity

- Are lighting, color, environment, clothing, and object emphasis similar enough?
- Does the result preserve the dominant visual priority of the source?

## Scoring Notes

Use issues and strengths consistently. Good examples:

- weak motion recreation
- generic faces
- incorrect camera energy
- over-cinematic output
- weak social-media realism
- incorrect lighting
- poor vibe recreation
- static-feeling motion
